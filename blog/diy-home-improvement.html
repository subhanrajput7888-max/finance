20 DIY Home Improvement Projects Anyone Can Do

March 20, 2026 16 min read Muhammad Subhan

You don't need to be a professional contractor to improve your home. Some of the most impactful upgrades are simple enough for complete beginners. I've compiled 20 DIY home improvement projects that require minimal tools, basic skills, and won't break the bank. Each project includes step-by-step instructions so you can tackle them with confidence.

1. Paint an Accent Wall

An accent wall transforms a room in a weekend for under $50. Choose a wall that naturally draws attention – usually the one behind your bed, sofa, or fireplace.

Steps:

  1. Clean the wall with mild soap and water
  2. Apply painter's tape to trim and ceiling
  3. Apply primer if covering dark colors
  4. Paint with a roller, working in W patterns
  5. Apply second coat after 2-4 hours
  6. Remove tape while paint is slightly damp

Cost: $30-60 | Time: 4-6 hours | Skill Level: Beginner

2. Update Cabinet Hardware

New knobs and pulls instantly modernize kitchens and bathrooms. This simple swap takes just an hour but makes a dramatic impact.

Steps:

  1. Measure existing hole spacing
  2. Purchase new hardware with same measurements
  3. Unscrew old hardware from inside cabinets
  4. Install new hardware, tightening by hand

Cost: $50-150 | Time: 1-2 hours | Skill Level: Beginner

3. Install Peel-and-Stick Backsplash

Modern peel-and-stick backsplashes look like real tile but install in hours without grout or special tools.

Steps:

  1. Clean wall thoroughly and let dry
  2. Measure and plan your layout
  3. Cut tiles to fit around outlets with utility knife
  4. Peel backing and press firmly to wall
  5. Use a roller to ensure good adhesion

Cost: $50-150 | Time: 2-4 hours | Skill Level: Beginner

4. Add Floating Shelves

Floating shelves add storage and display space with a clean, modern look. Perfect for bathrooms, kitchens, and living rooms.

Steps:

  1. Locate wall studs with a stud finder
  2. Mark shelf bracket positions with pencil
  3. Drill pilot holes and install brackets
  4. Slide shelf onto brackets and secure

Cost: $30-100 | Time: 1-2 hours | Skill Level: Beginner

5. Upgrade Light Fixtures

Replacing outdated light fixtures is one of the best ways to modernize your home. Always turn off power at the breaker before working.

Steps:

  1. Turn off power at circuit breaker
  2. Remove old fixture and disconnect wires
  3. Connect new fixture wires (black to black, white to white, ground to ground)
  4. Secure fixture to ceiling box
  5. Install bulbs and turn power back on

Cost: $50-200 | Time: 1-2 hours | Skill Level: Intermediate

6. Recaulk Bathroom

Old, moldy caulk makes a bathroom look dirty and can cause water damage. Fresh caulk looks clean and professional.

Steps:

  1. Remove old caulk with scraper and caulk remover
  2. Clean area thoroughly with rubbing alcohol
  3. Apply painter's tape on both sides of joint
  4. Apply new caulk in steady motion
  5. Smooth with wet finger or caulk tool
  6. Remove tape immediately and let dry 24 hours

Cost: $10-20 | Time: 1-2 hours | Skill Level: Beginner

7. Replace Faucet

A new faucet can update your kitchen or bathroom instantly. Modern faucets come with clear instructions for DIY installation.

Steps:

  1. Turn off water supply valves
  2. Disconnect supply lines and remove old faucet
  3. Clean sink surface
  4. Install new faucet according to instructions
  5. Reconnect supply lines and check for leaks

Cost: $50-300 | Time: 1-2 hours | Skill Level: Intermediate

8. Install Dimmer Switches

Dimmer switches add ambiance and save energy. They're perfect for dining rooms, bedrooms, and living spaces.

Steps:

  1. Turn off power at circuit breaker
  2. Remove switch plate and unscrew switch
  3. Disconnect wires from old switch
  4. Connect dimmer wires according to instructions
  5. Secure in box and install new plate

Cost: $15-40 | Time: 30 minutes | Skill Level: Intermediate

9. Create Shiplap Wall

Shiplap adds character and charm to any room. Use real wood boards or shiplap-style paneling for easier installation.

Steps:

  1. Measure wall and calculate materials needed
  2. Cut boards to length
  3. Locate studs and mark vertical lines
  4. Install boards from bottom up, using spacers
  5. Nail into studs at each board
  6. Fill holes and paint or stain

Cost: $100-300 | Time: 4-8 hours | Skill Level: Intermediate

10. Install Closet Organizer

A closet organizer doubles your storage space. Many systems are designed for easy DIY installation.

Steps:

  1. Empty closet and patch holes
  2. Plan layout with included template
  3. Install wall brackets and shelf standards
  4. Hang shelves and rods
  5. Add drawers and accessories

Cost: $100-500 | Time: 2-4 hours | Skill Level: Beginner

11. Refresh Landscaping

Simple landscaping updates dramatically improve curb appeal. Focus on the front entry for maximum impact.

Steps:

  1. Remove dead plants and weeds
  2. Edge garden beds with a spade
  3. Add fresh mulch (2-3 inches deep)
  4. Plant colorful annuals near entry
  5. Add solar lights along walkway

Cost: $50-200 | Time: 2-4 hours | Skill Level: Beginner

12. Update Mailbox

A new mailbox is an inexpensive way to boost curb appeal. Choose one that complements your home's style.

Steps:

  1. Remove old mailbox and post
  2. Dig new hole if needed
  3. Set post in concrete
  4. Attach mailbox after concrete cures
  5. Add house numbers for visibility

Cost: $30-100 | Time: 1-2 hours | Skill Level: Beginner

13. Replace House Numbers

Modern house numbers make your home easier to find and add style. Choose large, visible numbers.

Steps:

  1. Choose location and layout
  2. Mark hole positions with pencil
  3. Drill pilot holes
  4. Install numbers with included hardware

Cost: $15-50 | Time: 30 minutes | Skill Level: Beginner

14. Install Video Doorbell

Video doorbells add security and convenience. Most are designed for easy DIY installation.

Steps:

  1. Charge doorbell battery if applicable
  2. Download app and create account
  3. Remove existing doorbell or install new location
  4. Mount bracket and attach doorbell
  5. Connect to WiFi through app

Cost: $50-250 | Time: 30 minutes | Skill Level: Beginner

15. Add Weatherstripping

Weatherstripping seals gaps around doors and windows, reducing drafts and energy bills.

Steps:

  1. Clean door jamb thoroughly
  2. Measure and cut weatherstripping to length
  3. Peel and stick to door jamb
  4. Test door closes properly
  5. Add door sweep to bottom if needed

Cost: $10-30 | Time: 30 minutes | Skill Level: Beginner

16. Paint Ceiling

A fresh white ceiling makes rooms feel taller and brighter. Use ceiling paint for best results.

Steps:

  1. Remove or cover all furniture
  2. Apply painter's tape to walls
  3. Cut in around edges with brush
  4. Roll ceiling with extension pole
  5. Apply second coat if needed

Cost: $30-60 | Time: 4-6 hours | Skill Level: Beginner

17. Create Custom Doormat

A personalized doormat welcomes guests and adds personality to your entry.

Steps:

  1. Purchase plain coir doormat
  2. Create stencil or use painter's tape for design
  3. Secure stencil with tape
  4. Paint with outdoor acrylic paint
  5. Let dry completely before using

Cost: $20-40 | Time: 1-2 hours | Skill Level: Beginner

18. Hang Curtains Properly

Hanging curtains high and wide makes windows look larger and rooms feel more spacious.

Steps:

  1. Mount rod 6-12 inches above window frame
  2. Extend rod 6-12 inches beyond window width
  3. Use level to ensure rod is straight
  4. Hang curtains that touch or puddle on floor

Cost: $50-150 | Time: 1-2 hours | Skill Level: Beginner

19. Paint Tile

Painting tile is a budget-friendly alternative to replacement. Works best on vertical surfaces with low traffic.

Steps:

  1. Clean tile thoroughly with degreaser
  2. Sand lightly with fine sandpaper
  3. Apply bonding primer
  4. Paint with tile-specific paint
  5. Apply clear topcoat for durability

Cost: $50-100 | Time: 2-3 days | Skill Level: Intermediate

20. Update Outlet Covers

Replacing yellowed outlet covers with fresh white or decorative ones is a quick upgrade.

Steps:

  1. Turn off power at circuit breaker
  2. Unscrew old cover plates
  3. Install new covers with matching screws
  4. Turn power back on

Cost: $10-30 | Time: 30 minutes | Skill Level: Beginner

Essential DIY Tools for Beginners

  • Hammer
  • Screwdriver set (Phillips and flathead)
  • Tape measure
  • Level
  • Stud finder
  • Drill/driver
  • Utility knife
  • Adjustable wrench
  • Pliers
  • Safety glasses

Safety Tips for DIY Projects

  • Always turn off power before electrical work
  • Wear safety glasses when drilling or cutting
  • Use proper ventilation when painting
  • Know when to call a professional
  • Follow manufacturer instructions

Frequently Asked Questions

What DIY projects add the most value?

Painting, updating hardware, and improving curb appeal offer the best return on investment. These projects cost little but significantly improve appearance and perceived value.

What tools should every DIYer own?

Start with a hammer, screwdriver set, tape measure, level, stud finder, and cordless drill. These tools handle most basic DIY projects.

When should I hire a professional instead of DIY?

Hire professionals for structural changes, major electrical work, plumbing behind walls, and anything requiring permits. Safety should always come first.

How do I know if a project is too difficult for me?

If you don't have the right tools, feel unsafe, or the project requires skills you haven't developed, it's okay to hire help or start with simpler projects.

How much should I budget for DIY home improvements?

Set aside 1-3% of your home's value annually for maintenance and improvements. DIY projects typically save 50-70% compared to hiring professionals.

Can I really do these projects myself?

Yes! The projects in this guide are specifically chosen for beginners. Start with simpler projects and build your confidence and skills over time.

Conclusion

DIY home improvement doesn't have to be intimidating. Start with projects that match your skill level, invest in basic tools, and don't be afraid to learn as you go. Each project you complete builds confidence and saves money. Remember, even professionals started as beginners. Pick a project from this list and get started – your improved home awaits!
