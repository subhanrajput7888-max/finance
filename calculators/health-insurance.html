How the Health Insurance Cost Estimator Works

Health insurance premiums are influenced by several factors:

  • Age (older individuals typically pay higher premiums)
  • Geographic location (costs vary by region)
  • Tobacco use (smokers pay higher premiums)
  • Plan tier (Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum)
  • Family size (more members increase costs)

Under the Affordable Care Act (ACA), premium subsidies may be available based on income. The calculator provides estimates based on average costs for different tiers of coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the different health plan tiers?
Plans are categorized into four tiers: Bronze (40% insurer pays, 60% you pay), Silver (70/30), Gold (80/20), and Platinum (90/10). Higher-tier plans have higher premiums but lower out-of-pocket costs.
What is the difference between HMO and PPO?
HMOs require referrals to see specialists and primarily use in-network providers. PPOs offer more flexibility to see out-of-network providers but at higher costs. EPOs and HDHPs are other common plan types.
What is the open enrollment period?
Open enrollment typically runs from November 1 to January 15, during which you can enroll in or change health insurance plans. Special enrollment periods are available for qualifying life events like marriage, birth, or job loss.
