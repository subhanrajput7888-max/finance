How the ROI Calculator Works

Return on Investment (ROI) is calculated using the formula:

ROI = [(Final Value - Initial Investment) / Initial Investment] × 100

ROI is a performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency of an investment or compare the efficiency of different investments. It's expressed as a percentage and indicates the gain or loss relative to the cost of the investment.

While ROI is a simple and useful metric, it has limitations. It doesn't account for the time value of money or the duration of the investment. For investments held over different time periods, consider using annualized ROI for better comparison.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good ROI?
What constitutes a "good" ROI depends on the investment type and risk level. Historically, the stock market has returned about 7-10% annually, while bonds return 2-5%. Real estate, business investments, and other assets vary widely. Consider risk-adjusted returns when evaluating investments.
How is ROI different from annualized return?
ROI measures the total return over the entire investment period, while annualized return calculates the equivalent yearly return. For investments held over multiple years, annualized return provides a better comparison between different investments.
What are the limitations of ROI?
ROI doesn't account for the time value of money, investment duration, or risk. It also doesn't consider cash flows during the investment period. For more comprehensive analysis, consider metrics like Internal Rate of Return (IRR) or Net Present Value (NPV).
