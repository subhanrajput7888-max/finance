How the Loan Affordability Calculator Works

This calculator determines the maximum loan amount you can afford based on your income and existing debts. Lenders typically follow the 28/36 rule:

  • 28% rule: Housing expenses should not exceed 28% of gross monthly income
  • 36% rule: Total debt payments should not exceed 36% of gross monthly income

The calculator estimates the maximum monthly payment you can afford, then calculates the corresponding loan amount based on the interest rate and term you specify.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the debt-to-income ratio?
The debt-to-income (DTI) ratio is your monthly debt payments divided by your monthly gross income. Lenders typically prefer a DTI of 36% or less, with no more than 28% going toward housing expenses.
How do lenders determine loan eligibility?
Lenders consider your credit score, debt-to-income ratio, employment history, and down payment amount. A higher credit score and lower DTI ratio improve your chances of loan approval and better interest rates.
What if I can't afford the loan I want?
Consider options like increasing your down payment, extending the loan term, improving your credit score, or adjusting your desired loan amount. You might also explore different lenders or loan programs with more favorable terms.
