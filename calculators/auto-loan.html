How the Auto Loan Calculator Works

The monthly car payment is calculated using the formula:

MP = P[r(1+r)^n]/[(1+r)^n-1]

Where:

  • MP = Monthly payment
  • P = Loan principal (amount borrowed)
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12 and 100)
  • n = Total number of payments (loan term in months)

Auto loans typically have shorter terms (3-7 years) compared to home loans, resulting in higher monthly payments but lower total interest paid over the life of the loan. Consider your budget carefully before committing to a car loan.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good down payment for a car?
Aim for at least 20% down for a new car and 10% for a used car. A larger down payment reduces your monthly payments and total interest paid, plus helps you build equity faster.
How does loan term affect my car loan?
Longer terms mean lower monthly payments but more total interest paid. Shorter terms mean higher monthly payments but less total interest. Also, longer terms increase the risk of being upside-down on your loan (owing more than the car is worth).
Should I finance through the dealer or my bank?
  • Pre-approve with your bank or credit union first to know your best rate
  • Compare the dealer's financing offer with your pre-approved rate
  • Consider that dealers might offer rebates that make their financing more attractive
