How the Student Loan Calculator Works

The monthly student loan payment is calculated using the formula:

MP = P[r(1+r)^n]/[(1+r)^n-1]

Where:

  • MP = Monthly payment
  • P = Loan principal (total amount borrowed)
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12 and 100)
  • n = Total number of payments (repayment term in months)

Student loans typically have fixed interest rates and repayment periods of 10-25 years. Federal student loans often offer income-driven repayment plans that adjust payments based on your income and family size.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between federal and private student loans?
Federal loans typically offer lower fixed interest rates, income-driven repayment options, and loan forgiveness programs. Private loans are offered by banks and credit unions, often requiring a co-signer and having variable interest rates.
Can I pay off student loans early without penalties?
Yes, most student loans (both federal and private) allow prepayment without penalties. Making extra payments can save you thousands in interest over the life of the loan.
What are income-driven repayment plans?
These federal loan programs cap monthly payments at a percentage of discretionary income (usually 10-20%) and forgive remaining balances after 20-25 years of qualifying payments. Options include PAYE, REPAYE, IBR, and ICR plans.
