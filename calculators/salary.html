How the Salary Calculator Works

This calculator estimates your take-home pay by subtracting common deductions from your gross salary:

  • Social Security tax (6.2% of gross pay up to the wage base limit)
  • Medicare tax (1.45% of all wages, plus 0.9% additional Medicare tax on wages over $200,000)
  • Federal income tax (based on current tax brackets)
  • State income tax (varies by state, average estimated at 5%)
  • Other common deductions like retirement contributions

Note: This is an estimate only. Actual deductions may vary based on your location, tax filing status, number of allowances, and other factors. Consult a tax professional for accurate calculations.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between gross and net salary?
Gross salary is your total compensation before any deductions. Net salary (take-home pay) is what you actually receive after taxes and other deductions. The difference can be substantial depending on your income level and location.
What are common payroll deductions?
Common deductions include federal income tax, Social Security tax (6.2%), Medicare tax (1.45%), state income tax, local taxes, retirement contributions (401k), health insurance premiums, life insurance, and other voluntary deductions.
How can I increase my take-home pay?
Strategies include contributing to pre-tax accounts (401k, HSA), flexible spending accounts, adjusting withholding allowances, relocating to a state with no income tax, and negotiating a higher salary. Consult a tax advisor for personalized advice.
