How the Investment Growth Calculator Works

This calculator projects the growth of your investment using compound interest formulas. It accounts for both an initial investment and ongoing monthly contributions:

Future Value = Initial Investment × (1 + r)^n + Monthly Contribution × [((1 + r)^n - 1) / r] × (1 + r)

Where:

  • r = Monthly rate of return (annual rate divided by 12 and 100)
  • n = Total number of months (years × 12)

Compound interest allows your investment to grow exponentially because you earn returns not only on your original principal but also on the accumulated returns from previous periods. This "snowball effect" becomes more pronounced over longer time horizons.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the power of compound interest?
Compound interest is the concept of earning interest on both your original investment and the interest you've already earned. Over time, this creates exponential growth, with your money growing faster as the investment ages. Albert Einstein reportedly called it the "eighth wonder of the world."
How do I choose the right expected return?
Historical averages can guide expectations: stocks ~7-10% annually, bonds ~2-5%, savings accounts ~0.5-2%. Remember that past performance doesn't guarantee future results, and higher returns typically involve higher risk. Diversification helps manage risk.
How does time affect investment growth?
Time is a crucial factor in investment growth due to compounding. The longer your money stays invested, the more dramatic the effects of compound interest become. Starting early can make a significant difference in your final portfolio value, even with smaller initial investments.
