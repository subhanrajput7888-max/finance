How the Credit Card Interest Calculator Works

The calculator uses the formula for compound interest to determine how long it will take to pay off your credit card balance:

  • MP = Monthly payment

Credit card interest compounds monthly, making it important to pay more than the minimum payment to reduce the time it takes to pay off your balance and the total interest paid.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I only make minimum payments?
Making only minimum payments extends the time to pay off your balance significantly and increases the total interest paid. Minimum payments are typically 2-3% of the balance or a fixed amount ($25-$35), whichever is greater.
How can I pay off credit card debt faster?
Strategies include paying more than the minimum, using the debt snowball method (pay smallest balances first), debt avalanche method (pay highest interest rate first), balance transfers to lower-rate cards, or consolidating with a personal loan.
What is the minimum payment calculation?
Credit card companies typically calculate minimum payments as either a percentage of the balance (usually 2-3%) or a fixed amount (often $25-$35), whichever is greater. Some issuers also include past-due amounts and fees in the minimum payment.
