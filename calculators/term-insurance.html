How the Term Insurance Calculator Works

Term insurance premiums are calculated based on several factors including age, health, lifestyle, and coverage amount. Generally, younger and healthier individuals pay lower premiums. The premium is determined by:

  • Age of the insured (younger people pay less)
  • Sum assured (higher coverage costs more)
  • Term of the policy (longer terms may have different rates)
  • Gender (statistically, women live longer)
  • Smoking status (smokers pay higher premiums)

Term insurance provides pure life coverage without any savings component, making it more affordable than permanent life insurance options.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much term insurance do I need?
A common rule of thumb is 10-15 times your annual income, but consider your family's specific needs, outstanding debts, mortgage, children's education, and future expenses. Subtract any existing coverage and assets.
What's the difference between term and permanent life insurance?
Term insurance provides coverage for a specific period at lower costs, with no cash value. Permanent life insurance (whole life, universal life) covers you for life, builds cash value, but costs significantly more.
Can I convert term insurance to permanent insurance?
Many term policies offer a conversion option that allows you to convert to permanent insurance without a medical exam. This is valuable if your health changes during the term period.
