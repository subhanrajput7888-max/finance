How the Mortgage Calculator Works

The monthly mortgage payment is calculated using the formula:

M = P[r(1+r)^n]/[(1+r)^n-1]

Where:

  • M = Monthly payment
  • P = Loan principal (home price minus down payment)
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12 and 100)
  • n = Total number of payments (loan term in years multiplied by 12)

Note: This calculator provides an estimate of principal and interest only. Actual monthly payments may also include property taxes, homeowners insurance, and private mortgage insurance (PMI).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good down payment percentage?
A down payment of 20% is traditionally considered ideal as it avoids private mortgage insurance (PMI). However, many programs allow for lower down payments, sometimes as low as 3% to 5%.
How does loan term affect my mortgage?
Shorter loan terms (like 15-year) typically have lower interest rates and result in paying less total interest over the life of the loan, but higher monthly payments. Longer terms (like 30-year) have lower monthly payments but higher total interest.
What is PMI and when is it required?
Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is typically required when your down payment is less than 20% of the home's purchase price. PMI protects the lender if you default on the loan and can usually be canceled once your equity reaches 20%.
