How the Retirement Calculator Works

This calculator projects your retirement savings using compound interest formulas. It calculates the future value of your current savings plus ongoing monthly contributions:

FV = PV(1+r)^n + PMT Ã— [((1+r)^n - 1) / r]

Where:

  • FV = Future Value (retirement savings at retirement)
  • PV = Present Value (current savings)
  • PMT = Monthly contribution
  • r = Monthly rate of return (annual rate divided by 12 and 100)
  • n = Total number of months until retirement

The earlier you start saving, the more time your money has to grow through compound interest. Even small increases in monthly contributions can significantly impact your retirement savings over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I save for retirement?
A common guideline is to save 10-15% of your annual income. Start early to take advantage of compound growth. If you begin later, you may need to save a higher percentage. Consider your retirement goals, expected expenses, and other sources of retirement income.
What's the 4% rule?
The 4% rule suggests that you can withdraw 4% of your retirement savings in the first year of retirement, then adjust for inflation each year after. This strategy aims to make your savings last 30 years, but individual circumstances may require adjustments.
What retirement accounts should I use?
Take advantage of employer 401(k) matching first. Consider Roth vs Traditional accounts based on your current and expected tax brackets. Other options include IRAs, SEP-IRAs for self-employed, and taxable investment accounts for additional savings.
