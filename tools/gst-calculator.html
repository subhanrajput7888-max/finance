What is GST?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a consumption-based tax levied on the supply of goods and services. It's a comprehensive, multi-stage tax that has replaced multiple indirect taxes in many countries, simplifying the tax structure and making transactions more transparent.

Our GST Calculator helps you quickly calculate the GST amount and determine the final price, whether you're adding GST to a base price or extracting GST from an inclusive price.

How GST Works

GST is applied at each stage of the supply chain, with credit for tax paid at previous stages. Key aspects include:

  • CGST: Central GST (collected by central government)
  • SGST: State GST (collected by state government)
  • IGST: Integrated GST (for inter-state transactions)
  • GST Rate: Varies by product/service category

Formulas

GST Exclusive:
GST Amount = Original Cost × (GST Rate / 100)
Net Price = Original Cost + GST Amount

GST Inclusive:
GST Amount = Original Cost × [GST Rate / (100 + GST Rate)]
Net Price = Original Cost - GST Amount

Example Calculations

Example 1: Adding GST (Exclusive)

  • Original Price: $1,000
  • GST Rate: 18%
  • GST Amount: $180
  • Total Price: $1,180

Example 2: Extracting GST (Inclusive)

  • Total Price (GST inclusive): $1,180
  • GST Rate: 18%
  • GST Amount: $180
  • Original Price: $1,000

Common GST Rates

  • 0%: Essential items (food grains, fresh produce)
  • 5%: Household necessities, transport
  • 12%: Electronics, business class tickets
  • 18%: Most goods and services (standard rate)
  • 28%: Luxury items, cars, tobacco

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between GST inclusive and exclusive?

GST exclusive means the price doesn't include tax (tax is added). GST inclusive means the price already has tax included (tax needs to be extracted).

Who needs to register for GST?

Businesses with annual turnover above the threshold (varies by country) must register. In India, it's ₹40 lakhs for goods and ₹20 lakhs for services.

Can I claim GST credit?

Yes, registered businesses can claim Input Tax Credit (ITC) for GST paid on purchases, reducing their overall tax liability.

GST Filing Tips

  1. File returns on time to avoid penalties
  2. Keep all invoices and records organized
  3. Reconcile GSTR-2A with your purchases regularly
  4. Claim all eligible input tax credits
  5. Use accounting software for accuracy
  6. Stay updated on GST rate changes
