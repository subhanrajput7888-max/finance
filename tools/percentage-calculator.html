What is a Percentage Calculator?

A Percentage Calculator is a simple yet essential tool that helps you calculate what percentage one number is of another. Percentages are used everywhere - from calculating discounts and tax to understanding statistics and grades.

This tool eliminates manual calculation errors and gives you instant, accurate results. Whether you're a student, shopper, or professional, this calculator makes percentage calculations effortless.

How Does It Work?

The calculator uses a straightforward formula to determine the percentage:

  • Value: The part or portion you want to calculate
  • Total: The whole or complete amount
  • Percentage: What portion the value represents of the total

Formula

Percentage = (Value / Total) × 100

Where:

  • Value = The part you're measuring
  • Total = The complete amount
  • Result = Percentage value

Example Calculations

Example 1: Test Score

  • Marks Obtained: 85
  • Total Marks: 100
  • Percentage: (85 / 100) × 100 = 85%

Example 2: Discount

  • Discount Amount: $30
  • Original Price: $150
  • Discount Percentage: (30 / 150) × 100 = 20%

Common Uses

  • Calculate test scores and grades
  • Determine discount percentages
  • Calculate tax amounts
  • Understand statistics and data
  • Track progress and growth
  • Compare proportions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate percentage increase?

Use the formula: [(New Value - Old Value) / Old Value] × 100. For example, if a price increased from $50 to $65: [(65-50)/50] × 100 = 30% increase.

What if the total is zero?

Percentage cannot be calculated when the total is zero, as division by zero is undefined. Please ensure the total value is greater than zero.

Can percentages be more than 100%?

Yes! If the value exceeds the total, the percentage will be greater than 100%. For example, if you sold 150 units against a target of 100, that's 150% achievement.

Quick Percentage Tips

  1. 10% of a number = Move decimal one place left (10% of 250 = 25)
  2. 50% of a number = Divide by 2 (50% of 80 = 40)
  3. 25% of a number = Divide by 4 (25% of 100 = 25)
  4. 1% of a number = Move decimal two places left (1% of 500 = 5)
